### Equations for Dynamic Elastic Properties

Using ultrasonic sound speed measurements, we can calculate the dynamic elastic properties of homogeneous, isotropic, linear elastic solid materials:

#### 1. Shear Modulus ($G$)
The Shear Modulus represents the material's resistance to shear deformation and is calculated directly from the shear wave speed ($c_{\text{S}}$) and density ($\rho$):
$$G = \rho c_{\text{S}}^2$$

#### 2. Poisson's Ratio ($\nu$)
Poisson's Ratio represents the ratio of transverse contraction to longitudinal extension. It is determined from the ratio of longitudinal speed ($c_{\text{L}}$) and shear speed ($c_{\text{S}}$):
$$\nu = \frac{c_{\text{L}}^2 - 2c_{\text{S}}^2}{2(c_{\text{L}}^2 - c_{\text{S}}^2)}$$

#### 3. Young's Modulus ($E$)
Young's Modulus represents tensile stiffness (elastic resistance to stretching/compression). It is calculated from the Shear Modulus ($G$) and Poisson's Ratio ($\nu$):
$$E = 2G(1 + \nu) = 2\rho c_{\text{S}}^2 (1 + \nu)$$

---

### Variable Definitions:
* **$c_{\text{L}}$**: Longitudinal sound velocity (m/s)
* **$c_{\text{S}}$**: Shear sound velocity (m/s)
* **$\rho$**: Material Density ($\text{kg/m}^3$)
* **$G$**: Shear Modulus (GPa)
* **$\nu$**: Poisson's Ratio (dimensionless)
* **$E$**: Young's Modulus (GPa)
