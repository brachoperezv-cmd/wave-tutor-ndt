In Attempt 1, the Solver picked the first peak of the received wave trace. The Guardrail correctly flagged this as transducer ringing and electrical noise. When the high-voltage electrical pulse excites the transducer crystal, it causes immediate electromagnetic and acoustic leakage ("ringing") that shows up at the beginning of the trace ($t=0$). Any peak before the pulse finishes active transmission ({{cutoff}} µs) is electrical noise, not a traveling wave.
